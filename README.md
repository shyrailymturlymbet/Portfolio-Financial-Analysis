# Portfolio-Financial-Analysis
Portfolio-Financial-Analysis of FRDM amd TTD
Analytical Synopsis: Quantitative Portfolio Evaluation of TTD and FRDM
Demonstrated advanced financial modeling and statistical proficiency by conducting a comprehensive comparative analysis of The Trade Desk (TTD) and the Freedom 100 Emerging Markets ETF (FRDM) to evaluate their respective roles as Growth and Value indicators. By managing a robust dataset of historical weekly prices and dividends from Yahoo Finance covering the period from December 2025 to March 2026, I utilized advanced Excel functions to clean, format, and synthesize raw market data into actionable investment intelligence, ensuring consistency by removing unnecessary variables and handling missing values.
The core of the analysis involved calculating and interpreting key risk-adjusted metrics using rigorous business statistics methodologies. I identified that the Growth portfolio (TTD) yielded a superior mean return of 12.50% compared to the Value portfolio’s (FRDM) 11.01%. While the Growth assets exhibited significantly higher volatility—evidenced by a sample standard deviation of 17.57% and dramatic price fluctuations ranging from a high of $91.45 in August 2025 to a low of $22.88 in March 2026—I utilized the Sharpe Ratio to determine that the Growth portfolio still provided a superior risk-adjusted return (0.49 vs. 0.41 for Value).
Critically, I applied correlation and covariance methodologies to assess the relationship between these assets, uncovering a negative correlation (approximately -0.15 to -0.25). This inverse relationship, supported by data visualizations and trend analysis, indicates that FRDM and TTD often react differently to market triggers, making them highly effective components for a diversified portfolio aimed at mitigating unsystematic risk. This quantitative assessment culminated in a strategic conclusion: while the Growth portfolio offered higher risk-adjusted rewards, the inclusion of Value assets, which maintained a lower standard deviation of 17.15%, remains a fundamental requirement for balancing overall portfolio volatility and achieving long-term stability.
To sum up, the analysis of the Freedom 100 Emerging Markets ETF (FRDM) and The Trade Desk (TTD) over the studied period reveals a clear trade-off between risk and reward. While Growth portfolios (like TTD) achieved a higher mean return of 12.50%, they were accompanied by significantly greater dispersion and volatility, as evidenced by a higher sample standard deviation of 17.57%. Conversely, the Value portfolio offered more stability with a lower standard deviation of 17.15%, despite its slightly lower average return of 11.01%. The most critical takeaway for investors is the calculated negative correlation (approximately -0.15 to -0.25) between the two assets. This inverse relationship suggests that FRDM and TTD often react differently to market triggers, making them effective components for a diversified portfolio aimed at mitigating unsystematic risk. Ultimately, while the Growth portfolio provided a superior risk-adjusted return (Sharpe ratio of 0.49 vs. 0.41), the inclusion of Value assets remains essential for balancing overall portfolio volatility.
